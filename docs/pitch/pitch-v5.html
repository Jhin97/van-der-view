02 / 07 — The Problem

Sixty years of teaching chemistry on paper.

A 3D science taught with 2D tools is an epistemic bug, not a tooling one.

  • Chirality, induced fit, H-bond directionality — invisible on a chalkboard
  • Students draw flat rings on exams; they never intuit binding
  • Med-chem teams burn cycles on candidates a 3D inspection would have killed
  • The gap is not knowledge — it is embodiment

04 / 07 — Infrastructure

A coordination substrate for serious 3D work.

One dispatcher. Many specialist agent teams. One shared canvas.

  • Central dispatcher routes subtasks to domain-owned agent teams
  • Each sub-team is autonomous, reports back, stays in sync on the shared scene
  • Built once for chemistry; reusable for any 3D domain
  • This is what lets a small team ship the work of a large one

05 / 07 — Horizontal Generalization

The whole is greater than the sum of its parts.

The same agent-coordination + 3D scene engine ports straight into engineering, robotics, and any field where the geometry of small components decides the outcome.

  • Drug discovery is the wedge — the substrate is the moat
  • Wherever the parts are too small, too coupled, or too dangerous to disassemble, the same VR + agent stack applies
  • Robotics, mech-eng, semiconductor packaging, surgical planning — same primitives, different domain
  • Two growth axes from one origin: deeper into chemistry, wider into hardware
